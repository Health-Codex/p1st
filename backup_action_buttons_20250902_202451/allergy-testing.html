Our Allergy Testing Methods

Advanced techniques to identify your specific allergens

Skin Prick Testing

Quick and reliable testing method that introduces small amounts of suspected allergens to the skin surface to observe reactions.

  • Results in 15-20 minutes
  • Tests multiple allergens at once
  • Minimal discomfort
  • Highly accurate results

Blood Testing

Laboratory analysis that measures the amount of specific antibodies in your blood to identify allergic sensitivities.

  • Single blood draw
  • No direct exposure to allergens
  • Ideal for certain medications or skin conditions
  • Comprehensive allergen panel

Food Allergy Testing

Specialized testing to identify food allergies and sensitivities that may be causing digestive issues or other symptoms.

  • Common food allergen panels
  • Elimination diet guidance
  • Nutritional counseling
  • Personalized food plans

Environmental Allergen Testing

Comprehensive testing for common environmental triggers like pollen, dust mites, pet dander, and mold spores.

  • Seasonal allergen identification
  • Indoor allergen detection
  • Home environment recommendations
  • Avoidance strategies

What to Expect During Allergy Testing

Our simple, efficient testing process

1

Initial Consultation

Meet with our healthcare provider to discuss your symptoms, medical history, and suspected allergens. This helps us determine the most appropriate testing method for your specific situation.

2

Preparation

You'll receive instructions on how to prepare for your test, which may include temporarily stopping certain medications that could affect results. Our team will guide you through any necessary preparations.

3

Testing Procedure

Depending on the type of test, we'll either perform a skin test (applying small amounts of allergens to your skin) or draw blood for laboratory analysis. Skin tests provide immediate results, while blood tests may take a few days.

4

Results & Diagnosis

Your healthcare provider will review your test results with you, explaining which substances you're allergic to and the severity of each reaction. This information forms the basis of your diagnosis.

5

Treatment Plan

Based on your test results, we'll develop a personalized treatment plan that may include medications, lifestyle modifications, avoidance strategies, or immunotherapy to help manage your allergies effectively.

Allergy Treatment Options

Comprehensive solutions for allergy management

Medication Therapy

Prescription and over-the-counter medications to control allergy symptoms, including antihistamines, decongestants, nasal sprays, and eye drops tailored to your specific needs.

Immunotherapy

Allergy shots or sublingual tablets that gradually expose you to increasing amounts of allergens, helping your immune system build tolerance and reduce sensitivity over time.

Avoidance Strategies

Practical guidance on how to minimize exposure to your specific allergens through environmental controls, lifestyle adjustments, and preventive measures.

Dietary Modifications

For food allergies, we provide detailed elimination plans, safe food alternatives, nutritional guidance, and education on reading food labels to prevent allergic reactions.

Home Environment Optimization

Recommendations for reducing allergens in your home, including air purification systems, dust mite covers, cleaning protocols, and humidity control measures.

Emergency Action Plans

For severe allergies, we develop personalized emergency plans and prescribe epinephrine auto-injectors (EpiPen) with training on when and how to use them.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about allergy testing

Is allergy testing painful?

Skin prick testing causes minimal discomfort - most patients describe it as feeling like a slight scratch. Blood tests involve a standard blood draw, which may cause brief discomfort. Our team works to make the process as comfortable as possible, and the minor discomfort is temporary.

How should I prepare for allergy testing?

You may need to stop taking antihistamines and certain other medications 3-7 days before testing, as they can interfere with results. Our team will provide specific instructions based on your medications and the type of test being performed. It's important to continue taking asthma medications and other essential prescriptions unless directed otherwise.

How long does allergy testing take?

Skin prick testing typically takes about 30-45 minutes, including the time needed to observe reactions. Blood tests only take a few minutes for the blood draw, but results may take several days to come back from the laboratory. Your entire appointment, including consultation and results discussion, usually lasts about 1-2 hours.

Is allergy testing covered by insurance?

Most insurance plans cover allergy testing when it's deemed medically necessary. Coverage varies by plan, so we recommend contacting your insurance provider to verify your specific benefits. Our staff can help with insurance verification and explain any potential out-of-pocket costs before your testing appointment.

Can children get allergy testing?

Yes, allergy testing is safe and effective for children of all ages. For very young children, blood tests might be preferred over skin tests. Our providers are experienced in working with pediatric patients and will use age-appropriate approaches to make the experience as comfortable as possible for your child.
