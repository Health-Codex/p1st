What is Telemedicine?

Telemedicine allows you to connect with our healthcare providers through secure video visits, providing convenient access to medical care from the comfort of your home or office. Our virtual visits offer the same high-quality care you expect from in-person appointments, with added convenience and flexibility.

Conditions We Treat Through Telemedicine

Many common conditions can be effectively diagnosed and treated through virtual visits. These include:

Common Illnesses

  • Cold and flu symptoms
  • Sinus infections
  • Allergies
  • Sore throat
  • Ear infections
  • Pink eye
  • Respiratory infections

Skin Conditions

  • Rashes
  • Hives
  • Insect bites
  • Minor burns
  • Eczema
  • Acne
  • Poison ivy/oak

Chronic Conditions

  • Medication refills
  • Diabetes management
  • Hypertension follow-ups
  • Asthma management
  • Depression/anxiety
  • Thyroid disorders
  • GERD/acid reflux

Important Note

Telemedicine is not appropriate for emergency situations. If you're experiencing chest pain, severe bleeding, difficulty breathing, or other emergency symptoms, please call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room.

How Telemedicine Works

1

Schedule Your Visit

Book a telemedicine appointment through our online portal or by calling our office.

2

Receive Instructions

We'll send you a link and instructions for accessing your virtual visit.

3

Connect with Your Provider

At your appointment time, click the link to join the secure video call with your provider.

4

Receive Care

Your provider will evaluate your symptoms, provide a diagnosis, and recommend treatment.

5

Follow-Up

Prescriptions can be sent electronically to your pharmacy, and follow-up appointments can be scheduled if needed.

Preparing for Your Telemedicine Visit

Ensure you have a stable internet connection.

Use a smartphone, tablet, or computer with a camera and microphone.

Find a quiet, well-lit space for your appointment.

Have a list of your symptoms, medications, and questions ready.

If possible, have a thermometer and other basic medical devices on hand.

Log in 5-10 minutes before your scheduled appointment time.
