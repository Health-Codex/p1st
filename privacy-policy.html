Our Commitment

Privacy Policy

How we protect your personal and health information

Effective Date: January 1, 2024

People First Urgent Care ("People First," "we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy and safeguarding the personal and health information entrusted to us. This Privacy Policy explains the types of information we collect, how we use it, and the rights you have regarding your data when you use our clinical services, website, patient portal, and digital tools.

Information We Collect

What Data We Gather

We collect only the information necessary to provide exceptional care and operate our services responsibly.

Personal Identifiers

Information such as your name, date of birth, phone number, mailing address, email address, emergency contact details, and insurance information submitted through online forms or during an in-clinic visit.

Health Information

Medical history, symptoms, diagnostic results, treatment plans, and visit notes collected as part of your clinical care in accordance with federal and state regulations.

Digital Interactions

Technical data such as IP address, browser type, device identifiers, cookies, pages viewed, and appointment preferences gathered through our secure website analytics and scheduling tools.

How We Use Information

Purpose of Data Processing

We use your information only for legitimate business and medical purposes and never sell patient data.

  • Clinical Care: Diagnosing and treating medical conditions, coordinating follow-up care, and communicating test results.
  • Patient Services: Scheduling appointments, verifying insurance coverage, handling billing, processing payments, and managing patient portal accounts.
  • Operations & Quality: Monitoring service performance, training staff, improving our website, and complying with legal and regulatory requirements.
  • Safety & Security: Protecting against fraud, unauthorized access, and other harmful activities affecting patients, employees, or systems.
  • Communications: Sending visit reminders, health education, and service updates you have consented to receive.
Information Sharing

When We Disclose Data

We limit sharing of your information to trusted parties who support your care or help us run critical services.

  • Care Partners: Referring physicians, specialists, labs, imaging centers, and pharmacies involved in your treatment.
  • Service Providers: HIPAA-compliant vendors that supply electronic health records, payment processing, appointment scheduling, cloud hosting, or analytics tools under strict confidentiality agreements.
  • Legal Requirements: Public health reporting, court orders, insurance audits, and other situations required or permitted by law.
  • With Your Consent: When you authorize release of information to a family member, employer, school, or other third party.
Your Privacy Choices

Patient Rights & Controls

You retain important rights regarding access to and control over your health information.

Access & Copies

Request copies of your medical records, visit summaries, or billing statements by contacting our Health Information Management team.

Updates & Corrections

Ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete information in your records. We will respond within applicable legal timeframes.

Communication Preferences

Choose how we contact you for appointment reminders, lab results, or promotional messages. You may opt out of marketing emails at any time.

Restrictions & Confidentiality

Request limitations on how your information is shared or designate confidential channels for sensitive communications when feasible.

Data Protection

Safeguarding Your Information

Security is built into our technology, facilities, and staff practices.

  • All digital records are stored in encrypted systems that meet HIPAA, HITECH, and applicable state privacy standards.
  • Access to patient information is restricted to authorized team members with role-based permissions and ongoing privacy training.
  • We maintain secure networks, intrusion monitoring, and multifactor authentication to prevent unauthorized access.
  • Paper records are safeguarded in controlled areas and destroyed using secure shredding procedures when no longer required.
Website & Cookie Practices

Online Privacy

We use limited tracking technologies to enhance site performance and user experience.

  • Cookies and analytics tools help us understand how visitors interact with our website so we can improve navigation, content, and accessibility.
  • We do not use third-party advertising networks or sell browsing data. You may disable cookies in your browser; however, some features may not function as intended.
  • Online forms transmitting personal information are encrypted, and submissions are routed directly to our secure clinical systems.
Children's Privacy

Protecting Young Patients

We treat minors in collaboration with parents or legal guardians while respecting applicable privacy laws.

We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 without verifiable parental consent. Parents or guardians may review, update, or request deletion of their child's information by contacting us using the methods listed below.

Policy Updates

Changes To This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect regulatory changes or improvements to our practices.

  • When updates are made, we will revise the effective date and post the updated policy on this page.
  • Significant changes affecting how we use or share your information will be communicated through email, patient portal notifications, or signage in our clinics.
  • Continued use of our services after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
