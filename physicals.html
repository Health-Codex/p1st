Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our physical examination services

How often should I get a physical examination?

For most healthy adults, an annual physical is recommended. Children and adolescents may need more frequent exams, especially for school and sports requirements. Your provider can recommend the appropriate frequency based on your age, health status, and risk factors.

Do I need an appointment for a physical?

While walk-ins are welcome, we recommend saving your spot online or scheduling an appointment for physicals to minimize wait times. This is especially important for DOT/CDL physicals which may take longer than other types of examinations.

How long does a physical examination take?

Most routine physicals take 20-30 minutes. DOT/CDL physicals may take 30-45 minutes due to additional requirements. The exact time depends on your health history, the type of physical, and any additional tests needed.

Does insurance cover physical examinations?

Many insurance plans cover annual wellness exams at 100%. Coverage for school, sports, and employment physicals varies by insurance provider. DOT physicals are typically not covered by insurance and are paid out-of-pocket. Our staff can verify your benefits before your visit.

What's the difference between a DOT physical and a regular physical?

DOT physicals follow specific federal guidelines and must be performed by a certified medical examiner. They focus on conditions that might affect a driver's ability to safely operate a commercial vehicle, including vision, hearing, blood pressure, and certain medical conditions. Regular physicals are more general health assessments.
