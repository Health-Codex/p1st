Comprehensive Vaccination Services

Protecting individuals and communities through immunization

At People First Urgent Care, we offer a wide range of vaccination services to help protect you and your family from preventable diseases. Our experienced healthcare providers can administer vaccines for all age groups, from infants to seniors, in a comfortable and convenient setting.

Vaccinations are one of the most effective ways to prevent serious illnesses and their complications. By staying up-to-date with recommended immunizations, you're not only protecting yourself but also helping to prevent the spread of contagious diseases in your community.

Why Vaccinations Matter

Vaccines work with your body's natural defenses to build protection against specific diseases. When you get vaccinated, your immune system responds by:

  • Recognizing the invading germ (virus or bacteria)
  • Producing antibodies to fight the infection
  • Remembering the disease and how to fight it

This process helps your body develop immunity without having to get the disease first. Vaccines are thoroughly tested for safety and effectiveness before they're approved for use.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our vaccination services

Do I need an appointment for vaccinations?

While walk-ins are welcome, we recommend scheduling an appointment for vaccinations to minimize wait times. You can easily save your spot online or call our office to schedule a convenient time.

Are vaccines covered by insurance?

Most vaccines are covered by insurance plans, including Medicare and Medicaid. The coverage amount depends on your specific plan. We recommend contacting your insurance provider to verify coverage before your visit. For those without insurance, we offer affordable self-pay options.

Can I receive multiple vaccines in one visit?

Yes, in many cases, multiple vaccines can be administered during the same visit. Our healthcare providers will evaluate your specific needs and medical history to determine if it's appropriate for you. This approach can save you time and reduce the number of office visits needed to complete your immunizations.

What should I bring to my vaccination appointment?

Please bring your photo ID, insurance card (if applicable), and any vaccination records you may have. If you're receiving a follow-up dose of a vaccine, it's helpful to bring documentation of your previous dose. For children, please bring their immunization records.
