Care Options

When to Choose Urgent Care

Understanding the right level of care for your situation

Choose Urgent Care When:

  • You have a non-life-threatening illness or injury
  • Your primary care doctor is unavailable
  • You need care outside of regular office hours
  • You need X-rays or lab tests
  • You have a minor fracture or sprain
  • You have a cut that may need stitches
  • You have a fever without a rash

Go to the Emergency Room When:

  • You have chest pain or difficulty breathing
  • You're experiencing severe abdominal pain
  • You have a severe head injury
  • You're experiencing stroke symptoms
  • You have uncontrolled bleeding
  • You have a severe burn
  • You have a broken bone with deformity

If you are experiencing a life-threatening emergency, call 911 immediately.

Your Visit

What to Expect

Our simple, efficient process for urgent care visits

1

Check-In

Save time by checking in online before you arrive, or simply walk in and register at our front desk. We'll collect your basic information and insurance details.

2

Brief Wait

Our goal is to minimize wait times. Most patients are seen within 30 minutes of arrival. While you wait, you can complete any necessary paperwork.

3

Evaluation

A medical assistant will check your vital signs and gather information about your symptoms or injury. This helps us prepare for your examination.

4

Examination & Treatment

You'll be examined by one of our healthcare providers who will diagnose your condition and recommend appropriate treatment. This may include on-site tests or procedures.

5

Discharge & Follow-Up

Before you leave, we'll provide detailed instructions for your recovery and any necessary prescriptions. We'll also coordinate any needed follow-up care.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our urgent care services

Do I need an appointment for urgent care?

No appointment is necessary for urgent care visits. You can walk in anytime during our business hours. However, you can save time by using our online check-in system to "save your spot" in line before you arrive.

What insurance plans do you accept?

We accept most major insurance plans, including Medicare, Medicaid, and commercial insurance. Please visit our Insurance page for a complete list of accepted plans. If you don't have insurance, we offer affordable self-pay options.

How long will my urgent care visit take?

Most urgent care visits take between 30-60 minutes from check-in to discharge, depending on the complexity of your condition and any necessary tests or procedures. Our goal is to provide thorough care as efficiently as possible.

Can you prescribe medications?

Yes, our providers can prescribe medications as needed for your condition. We can send prescriptions electronically to your preferred pharmacy for your convenience. Please note that we follow strict guidelines for prescribing controlled substances.

What should I bring to my urgent care visit?

Please bring your photo ID, insurance card (if applicable), a list of current medications, and any relevant medical records or information about your condition. If you're bringing a minor child, please bring proof of guardianship.
